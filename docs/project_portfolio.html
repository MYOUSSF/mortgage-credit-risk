Methods

Modelling Methodology

Following Sexton (2022) with engineering improvements for scale and production-readiness.

Ch. 1 · PD Baseline
Weight-of-Evidence Logistic Regression
WoE_j = ln(p_j / q_j)
IV = Σ (p_j − q_j) · WoE_j
WoE transforms features into a common scale, handles non-linearity, and satisfies regulatory interpretability requirements. Maps are fitted on training data only — no leakage to OOS/OOT.
  • L2 regularisation (C = 1.0, liblinear solver)
  • class_weight='balanced' upweights defaults ~155×
  • Hosmer-Lemeshow calibration test (p > 0.05 = good fit)
  • Decile scorecard with default rates + capture rates
Ch. 2 · PD Ensemble
XGBoost with GPU Acceleration
tree_method="hist" + device="cuda"
scale_pos_weight = neg / pos ≈ 155
Captures non-linear interactions (FICO × CLTV × HPI change) missed by logistic regression. Histogram approximation reduces RAM by 5–10× vs exact splits.
  • 500 trees, max_depth=6, learning_rate=0.05
  • Early stopping on OOS AUC (rounds=20)
  • GPU: ~15× speedup on T4 vs CPU for 56M rows
  • Feature importance: gain, cover, frequency
Ch. 3 · LGD
Fractional Response Model
LGD_hat = σ(x'β) = 1 / (1 + e^(−x'β))
OLS on logit-transformed LGD with predictions mapped back via the sigmoid — guarantees LGD_hat ∈ (0, 1). The regulatory-preferred approach for bounded regression targets.
  • Interpretable coefficients in logit scale
  • Predictions always economically valid
  • Compared against splines, RF, and XGBoost
  • Metric: RMSE, MAE, R², mean bias
Validation
Leakage Prevention & OOT Testing
PSI = Σ (p̂_i − q̂_i) · ln(p̂_i / q̂_i)
PSI > 0.25 → Major population shift
Four-layer leakage prevention: WoE maps, imputer, and scaler all fitted on training data only; post-default rows dropped before the 12-month window; OOT split is strictly temporal.
  • OOT period: 2017–2024 (post-COVID rate cycle)
  • PSI computed for all features across Train/OOT
  • OOT degradation of ~3 AUROC points — expected
  • PSI spike on loan_age/rate: rate environment shift
Regulatory Alignment

Framework & Compliance Context

Models are designed to align with international regulatory requirements for internal credit risk estimation.

🏦
Basel II / III IRB
Internal Ratings-Based approach for regulatory capital. PD and LGD estimates feed directly into RWA calculation. Through-the-cycle training on 2000–2020 data satisfies EBA guidelines on economic cycle coverage.
📊
IFRS 9 / CECL
Forward-looking Expected Credit Loss provisioning. The 12-month PD aligns with Stage 1 (performing) ECL. The LGD models provide the loss severity component. ECL = PD × LGD × EAD.
🔬
Model Risk (SR 11-7)
Rigorous out-of-time validation, PSI monitoring, Hosmer-Lemeshow calibration testing, and score distribution analysis address model risk management requirements for regulatory-grade models.
